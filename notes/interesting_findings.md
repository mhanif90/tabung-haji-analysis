TH Annual report 2020



Summary (Own conclusion)



> The global health crisis resulting from the Covid-19 pandemic disrupted hajj process in 2020. Due to health concern, Saudi authority granted hajj permit only for a small portion of its citizen and barred pilgrimages from every other country, including Malaysia. In response, the Malaysian government decided to defer sending pilgrims to Makkah for the 1441H/2020M Hajj season to ensure the safety, security and health of Malaysian pilgrims. Despite this, the coronavirus has accelerated the importance and usage of technology and digital services for TH to remain relevant. The postponement of hajj also gave TH the opportunity to re-evaluate its hajj operations and plan for a post COVID-19 future by fine-tuning the five-year Hajj Blueprint 2021-2025 in order to further to improve the quality of hajj services and operations. To take TH to greater heights, TH have focused on six (6) main thrusts namely, Fund Administration, Hajj Management, Deposit Management, Governance, Human Capital and TH Group. 



TH concluded the year with a revenue of RM3.1 billion and a net profit after zakat of RM2.2 billion. Net profit rose 17.1 per cent or RM379 million for FY2020 from the RM1.8 billion recorded in 2019. TH’s total assets stood at RM82.5 billion, total fund exceeded by RM3.7 billion and allowing TH to declare a profit distribution rate of 3.10 per cent for FY2020 compared with 3.05 per cent paid in 2019, equivalent to a total pay-out of RM2.24 billion (4.5 per cent increase from the RM2.14 billion distributed in the previous year). The profit distribution rate was also higher than the average 12-month Islamic fixed deposit rate of 2.6 per cent. The stronger financial performance was primarily attributed to effective and sustainable investment strategies based on the Strategic Asset Allocation (SAA), which was formulated to enhance the organisation’s resilience against fluctuations in future economic cycles. With the postponement of hajj in 2020, TH contributed approximately RM32 million to assist and reduce the burden of affected groups, including medical and security frontlines who were on duty at hospitals and roadblock operations throughout the series of MCO, as well as help support local charitable causes on a sustained basis throughout FY2020.



TH online platform THiJARI began operations on 5 February 2020 that allowed TH depositors to manage their own TH accounts and other TH-related services such as fund transfers, hajj registration, accounts nomination, information on hajj flight schedules, and submit appeals to perform hajj through THiJARI, which helps to reduce waiting time and ease physical traffic at branch offices nationwide.
